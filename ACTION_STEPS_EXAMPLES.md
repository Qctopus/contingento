# Caribbean SME Action Steps - Examples

## üå¥ Communication Backup System

### Step 1: Create Printed Emergency Contact List
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **What:** Laminated cards with critical contacts
- **Who Includes:** JPS (Jamaica Public Service), NWC (National Water Commission), Jamaica Fire Brigade, JCF (Jamaica Constabulary Force)
- **Why:** "Hurricane Beryl 2024 proved digital contacts are useless when cell towers went down for days"
- **Cost:** JMD 500-2,000 (lamination at any print shop)
- **Done When:** Cards in 3 locations - office, vehicle, owner's home

### Step 2: Set Up WhatsApp Broadcast Groups
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **Why:** "During 2024 storms, WhatsApp worked when regular calls failed"
- **How:** Separate groups for staff, suppliers, key customers
- **Cost:** Free - just need smartphone with data
- **Local Tip:** Works on basic data/WiFi when calls don't

### Step 3: Get Backup Mobile Hotspot
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **Where to Buy:** Digicel or Flow stores
- **What:** Portable WiFi hotspot with prepaid data
- **Why:** "When main internet fails (common during storms), hotspot keeps payment systems running"
- **Cost:** JMD 3,000-8,000
- **Storage:** Keep charged in emergency kit

### Step 4: Install Two-Way Radios
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **Where to Buy:** Courts, Singer, local electronics stores
- **Why:** "Hurricane Beryl knocked out cell towers for 3+ days - radios work independently"
- **How Many:** 2-4 walkie-talkies
- **Cost:** JMD 6,000-15,000
- **Range:** Test for your property size

## üîß Equipment Maintenance

### Step 1: Create Equipment Inventory
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **What to List:** Refrigeration, generators, POS, cooking equipment
- **Info Needed:** Brand, model, purchase date, warranty, serial numbers
- **Why:** "Can't maintain what you don't track - 3x more expensive to fix than prevent"
- **Cost:** JMD 0 (use free Google Sheets or notebook)
- **Include:** Photos and receipts

### Step 2: Weekly Cleaning Schedule
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **When:** Every Friday
- **What:** Clean filters, check leaks, listen for unusual sounds, inspect belts
- **Time:** 15-30 minutes
- **Why:** "Jamaica's heat and humidity kill equipment fast - weekly cleaning extends lifespan by 50%"
- **Cost:** JMD 500-2,000 for basic cleaning supplies
- **Tip:** "A JMD 10,000 fridge repair avoided is profit saved"

### Step 3: Find Reliable Technicians
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **Who You Need:**
  - AC repair (critical in Jamaica heat)
  - Refrigeration tech
  - Electrician
  - Plumber
  - Generator service
- **How to Find:** Ask neighboring businesses for recommendations
- **Why:** "When AC fails in Jamaica heat, food spoils in hours - having contacts ready saves inventory"
- **Goal:** 2-3 technicians per equipment type with reviews noted
- **Cost:** JMD 0 (just collect contacts)

### Step 4: Monthly Professional Service
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **What:** Maintenance contracts for high-value equipment
- **Companies:** Cool Breeze, Arctic, local HVAC techs
- **Cost:** JMD 3,000-8,000/month (prevents JMD 50,000+ emergency repairs)
- **Priority:** Refrigeration first (protect inventory)
- **Example:** "Grocery stores that maintain fridges monthly rarely lose inventory - those that don't face JMD 200,000+ losses when compressor fails"

### Step 5: Equipment Replacement Fund
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **Amount:** Save JMD 5,000-10,000 monthly
- **Why:** "Commercial fridge costs JMD 150,000-300,000 - without savings, businesses close when equipment dies"
- **Alternative:** High-interest predatory loans at 30%+ (avoid this!)
- **Account:** Separate savings account
- **Start Small:** JMD 2,000/month if budget is tight
- **Reality:** "Fridge/AC lifespan is 5-8 years in Jamaica climate"

## üåä Flood Prevention

### Step 2: Improve Drainage (Medium-term)
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **What:** Clear drains, add French drains, slope ground away from building
- **Why:** "St. Elizabeth grocery store with good drainage stayed dry while neighbors lost JMD 500,000 in spoiled goods during 2023 heavy rains"
- **DIY Option:** Dig shallow trenches with gravel (JMD 3,000-8,000)
- **Professional:** French drains (JMD 40,000-60,000)
- **Local Tip:** Do this BEFORE rainy season when contractors are available

## ‚ö° Backup Power

### Step 1: Store Fuel Supply
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **Amount:** Keep 20-40 gallons gasoline or diesel in safe containers
- **Why:** "Spanish Town mini-mart with fuel storage was only store open during 3-day JPS outage - earned JMD 200,000 extra"
- **Storage:** Approved containers, cool ventilated area away from building
- **Rotation:** Use and replace every 3 months (fuel degrades in heat)

### Step 2: Buy Appropriate Generator
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **Size:** Calculate actual needs - fridge, lights, POS minimum
- **Common Mistake:** "Buying generator without calculating needs - too small to run business"
- **Where:** Local hardware stores, Courts, Singer
- **Cost:** JMD 35,000-50,000 (small inverter) to JMD 150,000+ (standby)
- **Must Have:** Transfer switch to avoid electrocution
- **Maintenance:** Test monthly - "it fails when you need it most"

## üî• Fire Detection

### Step 1: Install Smoke Alarms
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **What:** Battery smoke alarms in kitchen, storage, electrical areas
- **Where to Buy:** Any hardware store - JMD 2,500 each
- **Jamaica Fire Brigade:** Required for compliance
- **Critical:** "30% don't work when needed - change batteries every 6 months"
- **Kingston Restaurant Example:** "Fire detected in seconds - JMD 50,000 damage vs JMD 2 million total loss"

### Step 2: Get Correct Extinguishers
**Jamaica-Specific Context:**
- **Kitchen:** Class K for grease fires
- **Electrical:** Class C for equipment
- **General:** Class ABC for mixed use
- **Cost:** JMD 4,000-8,000 each
- **Training:** "Extinguishers sit unused during small fires without staff training"
- **Placement:** Near exits, visible, monthly pressure checks
